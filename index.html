    How to ace the ATS

    Applicant Tracking Systems filter resumes before a human ever sees them. Here's how Atelier Pro keeps you ahead.

    • Standard fonts only. We use Calibri, Arial, Georgia — fonts every ATS can parse without error.
    • Single-column layout. Multi-column resumes scramble information in ATS parsers. All our templates use clean single-column flow.
    • No graphics or icons. ATS bots ignore images entirely, wasting valuable keyword real estate.
    • Standard section headings. "Work Experience", "Education", "Skills" — not creative alternatives that confuse parsers.
    • Real bullet lists. Proper list markup, not emoji or unicode characters that get stripped out.
    • Mirror the JD. Use the exact phrases from the job description — "project management" vs "managing projects" are different matches.
    • Quantify everything. "Reduced load time by 60%" beats "improved performance significantly" on every ATS and recruiter screen.
    • Submit .docx first. Most ATS platforms parse Word documents more accurately than PDFs. Use our Word export unless specified otherwise.
    • Filename matters. Save as FirstName_LastName_Resume.docx — never "resume_final_v3_FINAL.docx".
    • Keep it honest. Keyword stuffing triggers manual review flags. Add only skills you genuinely have.
